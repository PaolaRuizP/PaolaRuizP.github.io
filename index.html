About Me.

I am a Biomedical Engineer from Universidad de Los Andes graduated Magna Cum Laude, currently pursuing my M.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering with an emphasis in Artificial Intelligence at the same university. I am a researcher at the Biomedical Computer Vision (BCV) research group and at the Center for Research and Formation in Artificial Intelligence (CINFONIA), under supervision of Prof. Pablo Arbeláez.

My main interests include drug development and discovery, molecular biology, and genetics, both in-vitro research and how to attend these problems with the aid of Machine Learning techniques. I collaborate in multiple projects with the Research Group in Nanobiomaterials, Cellular Engineering, and Bioprinting from the Biomedical Department of Los Andes University. Furthermore, I also work with a holistic understanding of surgeries, specifically on Endoscopic Surgeries and responsible and sustainable petroleum extraction employing automated Petrography.

I will be a Visiting Graduate Scholar at the Advanced Robotics and Computationally Augmented Environments (ARCADE) research group, led by Prof. Mathias Unberath PhD in JHU from January 2022 to September 2022.

Besides research, I love reading science fiction books and manual activities such as painting and bullet journaling.

Publications

Under the supervision of Prof. Pablo Arbeláez and Prof. Juan Carlos Cruz, my ongoing master’s thesis focuses on finding antimicrobial and translocator peptides to overcome microbial resistance. This research aims to merge deep learning with molecular dynamics simulations and in-vitro validation of in-silico results.
